Octopus


Octopuses are widely considered to be the most intelligent of all invertebrates (animals without a backbone or bony skeleton).

Each of t octopus's eight arms has a mind of its own.

Their arms are also covered in powerful suckers which have the ability to taste whatever they are touching.

Another interesting fact about octopuses is that they have three hearts and blue blood. Their blood, which is copper- rather than iron-based, is more efficient at transporting oxygen when water temperatures drop.








Giant Squid


The giant squid (Architeuthis dux) is a species of deep-ocean-dwelling squid in the family Architeuthidae.

The mantle of the giant squid is about 2 m (6 ft 7 in) long (more for females, less for males), and the length of the squid excluding its tentacles (but including head and arms) rarely exceeds 5 m (16 ft).

The giant squid is widespread, occurring in all of the world's oceans. Specimens are rare in tropical and polar latitudes.

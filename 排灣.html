Paiwan Slate House Introduction



Building


The Paiwan house is a gabled, asymmetrical building made of local slate and wood, stone slabs on the rooftop are stacked in a layering pattern, the front yard of a chief's house has a platform to issue public orders and host harvest festivals, there is a stone tablet with hundred pacer snakes carved on and a banyan tree symbol of the chief's honor and prosperity.









Male Stone And Female Stone


Male stone is harder, denser, and deep-colored, suitable for building houses.

Female stone is brittle and light-colored, good for building small walls or stairways.


When they build the house, there are male stones and female stones. The male stones are harder and denser slates.


The Ancestral Pillar


The ancestral pillar symbolizes the presence of an ancestral spirit, carved with totems of ancestral figures or snakes.








Why Do Paiwan People Want To Build A Slate House?


Paiwan people live by the river. They have a lot of stones. Therefore, they make good use of natural resources to build their house.


Paiwan Tribe


The Paiwan tribe has a strict social hierarchy, which is reflected in various features of the house.











Description Of The Activity


Use stone slabs to make a simple model of a slate house.


What We Should Do?


The model is first made from wooden planks, and then the stone slabs are glued with white glue.
